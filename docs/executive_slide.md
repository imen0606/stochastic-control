# Finance as an AI Training Ground

---

### Why Finance

Finance is one of the world's largest industries — yet AI is still limited to narrow tasks like fraud detection and credit scoring. The reason: financial decisions require **planning under uncertainty**, a capability today's AI lacks.

This makes finance the ideal domain to *train* that capability:

| | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Sequential by nature** | Every financial decision is a chain — enter, hold, adjust, exit — not a one-shot answer |
| **Uncertainty is the product** | Markets are noisy, incomplete, and adversarial — agents must reason about what they don't yet know |
| **Actions have real costs** | Trading, hedging, and rebalancing all carry costs — forcing the AI to weigh short-term expense against long-term payoff |
| **Mathematically verifiable** | Unlike most real-world domains, finance offers parametric models with provably optimal solutions — giving us ground truth to train and evaluate against |

The capabilities this builds — multi-step planning, cost-benefit reasoning, risk management under uncertainty — are **general-purpose**. They transfer to energy, supply chain, healthcare, and any domain where decisions are sequential and costly.

---

### What We're Building

A **suite of verifiable agentic environments** grounded in financial problems, where AI agents operate as autonomous decision-makers: observing markets, taking actions, receiving feedback, and adapting over time.

- **A family of tasks** spanning strategy activation, portfolio construction, execution optimisation, and risk management
- **Parametric difficulty control** — we generate unlimited training scenarios with known optimal solutions, tuning exactly which reasoning capability gets tested
- **Agentic by design** — multi-step interaction with a live environment, not static question-answering

We are building the equivalent of what math and code benchmarks did for reasoning — but for **planning and decision-making**.
